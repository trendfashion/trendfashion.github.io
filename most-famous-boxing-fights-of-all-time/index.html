The Thrilla in Manila

The Thrilla in Manila is one of the most famous boxing fights of all time. It was a heavyweight bout between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier that took place in 1975. The fight was held in Manila, Philippines, and was the third and final fight between the two fighters. The bout was dubbed "The Thrilla in Manila" by Ali, who described it as the closest thing to death he had ever experienced.

During the fight, both fighters took a lot of punishment, but it was Ali who eventually emerged victorious. The fight was closely contested and is still considered one of the greatest boxing matches of all time. It was a fitting end to the rivalry between two of the greatest heavyweight boxers of all time.

The Rumble in the Jungle

The Rumble in the Jungle was a heavyweight boxing match that took place in Kinshasa, Zaire, in 1974. The fight was between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman, who was the current heavyweight champion at the time. The fight was dubbed "The Rumble in the Jungle" by promoter Don King.

During the fight, Ali used a technique that he called the "rope-a-dope," which involved leaning against the ropes and letting Foreman tire himself out by throwing punches. This tactic worked, and Ali was able to knock out Foreman in the eighth round. The fight is still considered one of the greatest upsets in boxing history and is remembered for Ali's incredible comeback.

The Fight of the Century

The Fight of the Century took place in 1971 and was a heavyweight boxing match between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The fight was held at Madison Square Garden in New York City and was the first time that two undefeated heavyweight boxers had fought each other for the title.

The fight was closely contested, but it was Frazier who emerged victorious, winning by unanimous decision. The fight is still considered one of the greatest boxing matches of all time and is remembered for the incredible skill and determination shown by both fighters.

The Battle of the Sexes

The Battle of the Sexes was a boxing match that took place in 1973 between Billie Jean King and Bobby Riggs. The match was held at the Houston Astrodome and was a unique event in the history of sports. Riggs, a former tennis player, had claimed that women were inferior athletes, and King had agreed to play him in a boxing match to prove him wrong.

The fight was watched by millions of people around the world and ended in victory for King, who won by a score of 6-4, 6-3, 6-3. The Battle of the Sexes was a landmark event in the history of women's sports, and King's victory helped to pave the way for greater recognition and respect for female athletes.
