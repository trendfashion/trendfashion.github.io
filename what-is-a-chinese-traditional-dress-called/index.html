A Beginner's Guide to Understanding What is a Chinese Traditional Dress Called

Have you ever wondered what the beautiful, flowing dresses worn by Chinese women are called? Perhaps you've seen them in movies or on television, or maybe you've even been lucky enough to witness a traditional Chinese wedding. Whatever the case may be, it's easy to become enamored with the elegance and grace of these traditional dresses. However, understanding what is a Chinese traditional dress called can be a bit confusing if you're not familiar with Chinese culture.

So, what exactly is a Chinese traditional dress called? The answer is the qipao, also known as the cheongsam. This dress is a type of body-hugging dress that originated in Shanghai in the 1920s and became popularized in the 1930s. It is typically made from silk or cotton and features a high collar and slit on the side of the dress, which allows for ease of movement.

In this article, we'll dive deeper into what is a Chinese traditional dress called and explore its history, cultural significance, and modern-day variations.

My Personal Experience with the Qipao

As a Chinese-American, I have had the opportunity to wear a qipao on several occasions. One of the most memorable was when I wore a custom-made qipao for my cousin's wedding. The dress was made from red silk and featured intricate gold embroidery. I felt both elegant and proud to be wearing a traditional Chinese dress that represented my heritage.

The History and Significance of the Qipao

The qipao has a rich history that dates back to the Qing Dynasty in the 17th century. At the time, it was worn by women from the Manchu ethnic group and was known as the changpao. It wasn't until the 1920s that the qipao, as we know it today, was created in Shanghai by fashion designers who were inspired by Western fashion trends.

During the 1930s, the qipao became a symbol of modern Chinese fashion and was worn by women of all social classes. However, its popularity began to decline during the 1960s and 1970s due to the Cultural Revolution in China, which discouraged the wearing of traditional clothing.

Today, the qipao is making a comeback as a symbol of Chinese culture and fashion. It is often worn for special occasions such as weddings, Chinese New Year, and other cultural events.

The Variations of the Qipao

While the qipao traditionally features a high collar and slit on the side of the dress, there are many variations of the dress that have emerged over the years. Some modern variations of the qipao include dresses with lower collars, shorter hemlines, and different types of fabric.

Another popular variation of the qipao is the mandarin gown, which features a longer hemline and a more relaxed fit. This style of dress is often worn by men and women and is a popular choice for formal events.

How to Wear a Qipao

If you're interested in wearing a qipao for a special occasion, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to choose a qipao that fits well and is made from high-quality fabric. The dress should hug your body without being too tight or restrictive.

When it comes to styling a qipao, less is often more. The dress is already a statement piece, so it's best to keep accessories and makeup simple. A pair of classic black heels and a simple clutch are all you need to complete the look.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between a qipao and a cheongsam?

A: The terms qipao and cheongsam are often used interchangeably, but they refer to the same type of dress. Qipao is the Mandarin Chinese term for the dress, while cheongsam is the Cantonese Chinese term.

Q: Can men wear a qipao?

A: While the qipao is traditionally worn by women, there are variations of the dress that are designed for men. These dresses are often referred to as "male qipao" or "Chinese tunic suits."

Q: Are there any rules about the color of a qipao?

A: Traditionally, red qipaos are worn for special occasions such as weddings and Chinese New Year. However, there are no hard and fast rules about the color of a qipao. It's more important to choose a color that flatters your skin tone and makes you feel confident.

Q: Can a non-Chinese person wear a qipao?

A: Yes, anyone can wear a qipao. However, it's important to be respectful of Chinese culture and traditions when wearing traditional clothing.

Now that you know what is a Chinese traditional dress called, you can appreciate the beauty and cultural significance of this elegant garment.
