Understanding The Difference Between Tailored Fit And Regular Fit Shirts

Written by Eveline May 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to buying shirts, it can be overwhelming to choose the right fit. With so many options available in the market, it can be confusing to differentiate between tailored fit and regular fit shirts. However, understanding the difference can help you choose the right one that not only looks good but also feels comfortable.

Do you struggle to find the right shirt that fits your body type? Do you often feel uncomfortable in your shirt due to its fit? If your answer is yes, then this article is for you. We will discuss the difference between tailored fit and regular fit shirts to help you make an informed decision.

What is the Difference between Tailored Fit and Regular Fit Shirts?

The primary difference between tailored fit and regular fit shirts is their cut. A regular fit shirt has a standard cut that is designed to fit most body types. On the other hand, a tailored fit shirt is more form-fitting and is designed to fit closer to the body. It follows the natural contours of the body, giving a more polished and tailored look.

Another difference is the amount of fabric used in both types of shirts. A regular fit shirt has more fabric around the chest, waist, and arms, making it a bit loose. In contrast, a tailored fit shirt has less fabric, making it more fitted and snug around the body.

A tailored fit shirt is ideal for those with a slim or athletic build. It provides a flattering fit that accentuates their body shape. On the other hand, a regular fit shirt is suitable for those with a broader body type as it offers more room around the chest and waist.

My Personal Experience with Tailored Fit and Regular Fit Shirts

As someone who has a slim build, I have always found it challenging to find the right fit when it comes to shirts. Regular fit shirts often feel too loose, and I end up looking like I am wearing someone else's clothes. However, when I tried on a tailored fit shirt for the first time, I was amazed at how well it fit my body. It felt like it was made just for me, and the overall look was more polished and put together.

On the other hand, I have also worn regular fit shirts, and while they are comfortable, they don't give the same polished look as a tailored fit shirt. They tend to bunch up around the waist and arms, giving an unkempt appearance.

Which One Should You Choose?

The choice between tailored fit and regular fit shirts depends on your body type and personal preference. If you have a slim or athletic build, a tailored fit shirt would be more suitable for you. It provides a flattering fit that accentuates your body shape. On the other hand, if you have a broader body type, a regular fit shirt would be more comfortable and offer more room around the chest and waist.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Shirt Fit

When choosing a shirt fit, there are a few factors you should consider:

  • Body type: Your body type plays a crucial role in determining the right shirt fit for you. Consider your body shape and choose a fit that flatters your body type.
  • Comfort: It is essential to choose a shirt that feels comfortable to wear. A shirt that is too tight or too loose can be uncomfortable to wear.
  • Occasion: The occasion also plays a role in determining the right shirt fit. For formal occasions, a tailored fit shirt would be more appropriate, while a regular fit shirt would be suitable for casual occasions.

How to Ensure a Perfect Shirt Fit

To ensure a perfect shirt fit, follow these tips:

  • Take Your Measurements: Get your measurements taken by a professional tailor to ensure a perfect fit.
  • Try Before You Buy: Always try on a shirt before buying it to ensure a good fit.
  • Check the Shoulder Seams: The shoulder seams of the shirt should align with your shoulder bone.
  • Check the Sleeve Length: The sleeves of the shirt should end at your wrist bone.
  • Check the Collar: The collar of the shirt should fit snugly around your neck without feeling tight.

Question and Answer

Q. Is a tailored fit shirt more expensive than a regular fit shirt?

A. It depends on the brand and the material used. However, generally, a tailored fit shirt is more expensive than a regular fit shirt.

Q. Can I wear a tailored fit shirt if I have a broader body type?

A. While a tailored fit shirt is ideal for those with a slim or athletic build, you can still wear one if you have a broader body type. However, make sure to choose a size that fits you well and does not feel too tight.

Q. Can I alter a regular fit shirt to make it more fitted?

A. Yes, you can alter a regular fit shirt to make it more fitted. However, it is recommended to get it done by a professional tailor to ensure a perfect fit.

Q. How often should I replace my shirts?

A. It depends on how often you wear them and how well you take care of them. However, on average, shirts last for about two to three years.

Conclusion of What is the Difference between Tailored Fit and Regular Fit Shirts

Choosing the right shirt fit is essential for both comfort and style. While regular fit shirts are suitable for those with a broader body type, tailored fit shirts offer a more polished and tailored look. By considering your body type and personal preference, you can choose the right shirt fit that not only looks good but also feels comfortable to wear.
