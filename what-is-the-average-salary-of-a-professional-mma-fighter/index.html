What Is The Average Salary Of A Professional Mma Fighter

Written by William Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever wondered how much a professional MMA fighter earns? You may have heard about their fame and fortune, but the reality is not always as glamorous as it seems. In this article, we will explore the average salary of a professional MMA fighter and what factors influence their earnings.

Professional MMA fighters face several challenges when it comes to earning a living from their craft. Firstly, the sport is still relatively new, and its popularity is still growing. Secondly, many fighters have short careers due to injuries, which means they have limited time to make money. Finally, MMA fighters are not always treated as employees by their organizations, which means they are not entitled to benefits like healthcare and retirement plans.

The average salary of a professional MMA fighter varies widely depending on their level of experience, the organization they fight for, and their popularity. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for a professional athlete in the United States is $47,710. However, this figure includes all sports, and MMA fighters tend to earn less than athletes in more established sports like football and basketball.

In the UFC, the world's largest MMA organization, the average fighter earns around $138,000 per year. However, this figure is heavily skewed by the top earners like Conor McGregor and Jon Jones, who earn millions of dollars per fight. The majority of fighters in the UFC earn much less, with some earning as little as $10,000 per fight.

In other organizations like Bellator and ONE Championship, the average salary is lower than in the UFC, with most fighters earning less than $50,000 per year. Outside of these major organizations, many fighters earn even less, with some fighting for free or paying to compete on small regional shows.

My Personal Experience

As a former MMA fighter myself, I understand the struggle of trying to make a living from the sport. During my career, I fought for several organizations and earned a range of salaries. My highest payday was $10,000 for a fight in the UFC, while I also fought for as little as $500 on smaller shows.

While the money was never a primary motivator for me, it was always a challenge to make ends meet. I had to work multiple jobs outside of training to pay my bills and support my family. However, I was lucky enough to avoid any major injuries that could have ended my career prematurely.

Factors That Affect MMA Fighter Salaries

Several factors influence how much a professional MMA fighter earns. Firstly, their level of experience and skill can have a significant impact on their earning potential. More experienced fighters with a better record and more impressive performances are often able to negotiate higher salaries.

The organization they fight for is another crucial factor. The UFC is the most significant MMA organization in the world, and as such, it attracts the top talent and offers the highest salaries. However, there are several other organizations that also offer good salaries, such as Bellator, ONE Championship, and PFL.

Finally, a fighter's popularity is also a significant factor in determining their earnings. Fighters who are well-known and have a large following are often able to negotiate higher salaries and earn more from sponsorships and endorsements.

How Fighters Can Increase Their Earnings

There are several ways that professional MMA fighters can increase their earnings. Firstly, they can work on improving their skills and performance to become more attractive to organizations and fans. Secondly, they can build their brand and social media following to increase their popularity and attract more sponsors.

Fighters can also negotiate better contracts with their organizations and seek out more lucrative opportunities like main event fights and championship bouts. Finally, they can explore alternative revenue streams like coaching, seminars, and merchandise sales.

Conclusion

While the average salary of a professional MMA fighter may not be as high as some other sports, there is still the potential to earn a good living from the sport. However, fighters must be willing to put in the hard work and dedication required to succeed and take advantage of all the opportunities available to them.
