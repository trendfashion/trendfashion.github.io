How To Put Laces In Adidas Shoes A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Adidas shoes are a popular choice for athletes and fashion enthusiasts alike, but many people struggle with putting the laces in correctly. Whether you're new to Adidas shoes or have been wearing them for years, learning how to put the laces in properly can make a big difference in the fit and comfort of your shoes.

If you've ever struggled with getting your Adidas shoes to fit just right, you're not alone. Many people find that their shoes feel too tight or too loose, even after adjusting the laces. Fortunately, putting the laces in correctly can help solve this problem and make your shoes more comfortable to wear.

Here's a step-by-step guide to putting laces in Adidas shoes:

Step 1: Choose the Right Laces

Before you start lacing up your Adidas shoes, make sure you have the right laces for the job. Adidas shoes typically come with flat laces that are about 36 inches long, but you can also use round laces or longer laces if you prefer. Just make sure the laces are made of a durable material that won't fray or break easily.

Step 2: Remove the Old Laces

If your Adidas shoes still have old laces in them, you'll need to remove them before you can put in new ones. To do this, simply untie the laces and pull them out of the shoe. If the laces are knotted or difficult to remove, you can use a pair of scissors to cut them out.

Step 3: Thread the Laces

Begin by threading one end of the lace through the bottom eyelet on the left side of the shoe. Pull the lace through until it is even on both sides. Then, thread the other end of the lace through the bottom eyelet on the right side of the shoe and pull it through until it is even with the other side.

Step 4: Criss-Cross the Laces

Take the left lace and cross it over the right lace, threading it through the second eyelet on the right side of the shoe. Then, take the right lace and cross it over the left lace, threading it through the second eyelet on the left side of the shoe. Pull the laces tight to ensure a snug fit.

Step 5: Continue Criss-Crossing

Continue criss-crossing the laces up the shoe, threading them through each set of eyelets until you reach the top. Make sure the laces are pulled tight each time to ensure a secure fit.

Step 6: Tie the Laces

Once you reach the top of the shoe, tie the laces in a knot to keep them in place. You can also tie a bow if you prefer a more secure fit. Make sure the laces are tied snugly but not too tight, as this can cause discomfort and restrict blood flow to your feet.

By following these simple steps, you can put the laces in your Adidas shoes correctly and ensure a comfortable, secure fit. Whether you're hitting the gym or just running errands, properly lacing your shoes can make a big difference in how they feel and perform.

How to Adjust Laces for a Better Fit

While lacing your Adidas shoes properly is important, it's also important to know how to adjust the laces for a better fit. If your shoes feel too tight or too loose, you can use these tips to adjust the laces and find the right fit:

Option 1: Loosen the Laces

If your Adidas shoes feel too tight, you can loosen the laces by untying the knot and pulling the laces apart. This will create more space in the shoe and allow your feet to breathe. You can also loosen specific areas of the shoe by adjusting the laces in those areas.

Option 2: Tighten the Laces

If your Adidas shoes feel too loose, you can tighten the laces by pulling them tighter and tying them in a tighter knot. This will help keep your feet secure and prevent them from sliding around in the shoe. You can also tighten specific areas of the shoe by adjusting the laces in those areas.

Pro Tip: Use Lock Laces

If you're looking for a more secure and customizable fit, consider using lock laces. These laces come with a locking mechanism that allows you to adjust the tension of the laces and keep them in place without having to tie a knot. This can be especially helpful for athletes who need a secure fit that won't come undone during a game or workout.

Questions and Answers

Q: Can I use different types of laces in my Adidas shoes?

A: Yes, you can use different types of laces in your Adidas shoes as long as they are the right length and made of a durable material. Flat laces are the most common type of lace for Adidas shoes, but you can also use round laces or longer laces if you prefer.

Q: How tight should I tie my Adidas shoe laces?

A: You should tie your Adidas shoe laces snugly but not too tight. If the laces are too loose, your feet will slide around in the shoe and you may experience discomfort or blisters. If the laces are too tight, they can restrict blood flow to your feet and cause discomfort or pain.

Q: How can I tell if my Adidas shoes are the right size?

A: To determine if your Adidas shoes are the right size, there should be about a thumb's width of space between the end of your longest toe and the end of the shoe. Your heel should also fit snugly in the back of the shoe without slipping out.

Q: How often should I replace my Adidas shoe laces?

A: It's a good idea to replace your Adidas shoe laces every 6-12 months, depending on how often you wear them and how much wear and tear they experience. This will ensure that your laces are always in good condition and won't break or fray easily.

Conclusion of How to Put Laces in Adidas Shoes

Putting the laces in your Adidas shoes correctly is an important step in ensuring a comfortable, secure fit. By following these simple steps and using these tips to adjust the laces as needed, you can get the most out of your Adidas shoes and enjoy them for years to come.
