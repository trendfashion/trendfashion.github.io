How To Make Authentic Italian Pizza A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of ordering pizza from your local restaurant and not getting the authentic Italian taste you crave? Making authentic Italian pizza can be a challenge, but it's definitely worth it. Not only will you be able to customize your pizza to your liking, but you'll also impress your friends and family with your culinary skills. In this tutorial, we'll guide you through the process of making authentic Italian pizza from scratch.

When it comes to making authentic Italian pizza, the two most crucial elements are the dough and the sauce. The dough should be soft, chewy, and flavorful, while the sauce should be made with fresh, high-quality ingredients. It's important to take your time with each step of the process to ensure that your pizza turns out just right.

The target of this tutorial is to provide you with a step-by-step guide to making authentic Italian pizza at home. We'll cover everything from making the dough and sauce to assembling and baking your pizza.

In this tutorial, we'll cover the following main points:

  1. Making the dough
  2. Making the sauce
  3. Assembling and baking the pizza

Making the Dough

When it comes to making authentic Italian pizza, the dough is key. Here's our recipe for making the perfect dough:

Ingredients:

  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast.
  2. Add the warm water and olive oil, and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about an hour.

Making the Sauce

The sauce is just as important as the dough when it comes to making authentic Italian pizza. Here's our recipe for a basic tomato sauce:

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the garlic and sauté for about a minute, until fragrant.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, salt, sugar, oregano, basil, and black pepper.
  4. Lower the heat, and let the sauce simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Assembling and Baking the Pizza

Now that you have your dough and sauce ready, it's time to assemble and bake your pizza. Here's how:

Ingredients:

  • 1 batch of pizza dough
  • 1 batch of tomato sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Any additional toppings you desire (pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F.
  2. Roll out your dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness and shape.
  3. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving about an inch of space around the edges.
  4. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce, followed by any additional toppings you desire.
  5. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the toppings.
  6. Bake the pizza for about 12-15 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Additional Tips

- For an even crispier crust, place your pizza stone in the oven while it's preheating, and then transfer your pizza onto the stone to bake.

- Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best taste.

- Don't overload your pizza with toppings, as this can make the crust soggy.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I make the dough and sauce ahead of time?

A: Yes, you can make the dough and sauce ahead of time and store them in the refrigerator until you're ready to assemble and bake your pizza.

Q: Can I freeze the dough?

A: Yes, you can freeze the dough for up to three months. Just make sure to thaw it in the refrigerator overnight before using it.

Q: What's the best type of cheese to use?

A: Mozzarella cheese is the traditional choice for pizza, but you can also use other types of cheese, such as provolone, fontina, or goat cheese, depending on your taste preferences.

Q: How can I make my pizza healthier?

A: You can make your pizza healthier by using whole-wheat flour instead of all-purpose flour for the dough, and by using less cheese and more vegetables for the toppings.

Conclusion of Make Authentic Italian Pizza

Making authentic Italian pizza can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following these simple steps, you can create a pizza that's not only delicious but also authentic. Remember to take your time with each step, and use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best taste. We hope this tutorial has inspired you to make your own authentic Italian pizza at home!
