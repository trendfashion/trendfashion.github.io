Standard Vs Asian Fit What s The Difference

Written by William Sep 09, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of trying on glasses that just don't fit right? Do you constantly have to adjust your frames to keep them from sliding down your nose or digging into your temples? If so, you may be experiencing the frustration of finding the right fit for your face. This is where the concept of standard vs asian fit comes in.

The struggle to find the perfect glasses or sunglasses can be a real pain, especially if you have a unique facial structure. Standard frames are designed to fit a wider variety of face shapes and sizes, but they may not always work for those with flatter noses or higher cheekbones, which are more common in Asian populations. This is where asian fit frames come in.

Asian fit frames are designed with features such as shorter nose pads, wider frames, and reduced curvature to better fit those with flatter facial features. These frames can provide a more comfortable and secure fit, which is especially important for those who wear glasses or sunglasses for extended periods of time.

In summary, standard vs asian fit refers to the different designs of glasses frames that cater to different facial structures. Standard frames are designed to fit a wider variety of face shapes and sizes, while asian fit frames are designed with features that better accommodate those with flatter facial features.

How Standard vs Asian Fit Affects Your Comfort

As someone with flatter facial features, I used to have a lot of trouble finding glasses that fit comfortably. I would constantly have to adjust my frames to keep them from sliding down my nose or digging into my temples. It wasn't until I discovered asian fit frames that I finally found a comfortable and secure fit.

Asian fit frames have shorter nose pads that sit higher on the bridge of your nose, which helps to prevent slipping. These features work together to provide a more comfortable and secure fit, which is especially important for those who wear glasses or sunglasses for extended periods of time.

The Benefits of Asian Fit Frames

One of the main benefits of asian fit frames is the improved comfort they provide. By accommodating the unique facial features of those with flatter facial structures, asian fit frames can provide a more secure and comfortable fit. This can help to reduce slipping, pressure points, and discomfort, which can be especially important for those who wear glasses or sunglasses for extended periods of time.

In addition to improved comfort, asian fit frames also tend to have a more stylish and modern look. They often feature sleek lines and bold colors that are perfect for those who want to make a statement with their glasses or sunglasses.

How to Choose the Right Fit for You

When it comes to choosing the right fit for you, it's important to consider your facial structure and personal preferences. If you have flatter facial features and have had trouble finding glasses that fit comfortably in the past, asian fit frames may be a good option for you. On the other hand, if you have a more traditional facial structure, standard frames may work just fine.
