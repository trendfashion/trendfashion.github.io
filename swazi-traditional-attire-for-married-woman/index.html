The Umhlanga Reed Dance

One of the most iconic events in Swazi culture is the Umhlanga Reed Dance, also known as the Swazi Royal Reed Dance. This annual event is held in late August or early September and is a celebration of Swazi womanhood and virginity. During the event, thousands of young Swazi women gather together to cut reeds, which are then presented to the queen mother and used to reinforce the boundaries of the royal compound.

One of the most important elements of the Umhlanga Reed Dance is traditional attire. Women wear brightly-colored beaded skirts, called imbali, as well as colorful shawls and headbands. The colors and patterns of these garments hold special significance, representing everything from cultural identity to personal beliefs and values.
