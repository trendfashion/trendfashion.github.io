When it comes to buying athletic shoes and apparel, Nike is one of the most popular brands in the world. However, there has been a lot of controversy surrounding the company's labor practices, which has led many people to question whether or not Nike is a fair trade company.

One of the biggest pain points related to Nike's labor practices is the fact that the company has been accused of using sweatshop labor in the past. Sweatshops are factories where workers are paid very low wages and are forced to work long hours in poor conditions. This has led to concerns about the rights of Nike's workers, and whether or not they are being treated fairly.

The answer to the question of whether or not Nike is a fair trade company is not a simple one. What is Fair Trade?

Fair trade is a movement that aims to promote better working conditions and higher wages for workers in developing countries. The idea is that by paying workers a fair wage, they will be able to improve their standard of living and break the cycle of poverty. Fair trade also promotes sustainability and environmental responsibility. Companies that participate in fair trade are held to certain standards, which are designed to ensure that workers are treated fairly and that the environment is protected.
