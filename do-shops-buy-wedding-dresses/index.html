Do Shops Buy Wedding Dresses A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you wondering if shops buy wedding dresses? It can be a daunting task to figure out what to do with your wedding dress once the big day is over. Maybe you're looking to recoup some of the cost, or perhaps you want to donate it to a good cause. Whatever your reason, we've got you covered.

The short answer is yes, shops do buy wedding dresses. However, the process can be a bit more complicated than you might think.

When it comes to selling your wedding dress, there are a few different options available to you. You can sell it online through a marketplace like eBay or Facebook Marketplace, consign it at a local bridal shop, or sell it directly to a bridal shop.

So, let's dive into the details of each option and what you need to know about selling your wedding dress.

Selling Your Dress Online

One option for selling your wedding dress is to list it for sale online. There are a variety of websites and marketplaces that allow you to sell used wedding dresses, such as Stillwhite, PreOwnedWeddingDresses, and OnceWed.

Personally, I sold my wedding dress on Stillwhite and had a great experience. The process was simple, and I was able to recoup a decent amount of the cost. However, it's important to keep in mind that you'll need to handle the shipping and any potential returns yourself.

Consignment at a Local Bridal Shop

Another option is to consign your dress at a local bridal shop. This means that the shop will sell your dress on your behalf, and you'll receive a percentage of the final sale price.

This option can be a bit more convenient than selling your dress online, as the shop will handle the sales process for you. However, keep in mind that the shop will take a percentage of the sale price as their commission. Additionally, they may have specific guidelines or requirements for the dresses they accept for consignment.

Selling Directly to a Bridal Shop

Finally, you can also sell your dress directly to a bridal shop. This option may be the easiest, as you can simply bring your dress in and receive an offer on the spot. However, keep in mind that you may not receive as much money for your dress as you would through other methods.

Factors to Consider When Selling Your Dress

Regardless of which option you choose, there are a few factors to keep in mind when selling your wedding dress:

  • Condition: The better condition your dress is in, the more money you'll be able to get for it.
  • Age: Generally speaking, newer dresses will be worth more than older dresses.
  • Designer: Dresses from well-known designers will typically be worth more than those from less well-known designers.

Tips for Maximizing Your Sale Price

If you're looking to get the most money possible for your wedding dress, there are a few things you can do to maximize its sale price:

  • Clean and preserve your dress before selling it. This will help to maintain its condition.
  • Include high-quality photos in your online listings.
  • Be willing to negotiate on price, but don't sell yourself short.

FAQs About Selling Your Wedding Dress

Q: How much money can I expect to get for my wedding dress?

A: The amount you can expect to get for your wedding dress will vary based on a variety of factors, such as the designer, age, and condition of the dress. However, you can typically expect to recoup around 50% of the original cost.

Q: Is it better to sell my dress online or through a bridal shop?

A: The best option for selling your wedding dress will depend on your priorities. If you're looking for convenience, selling through a bridal shop may be the best option. However, if you're looking to maximize your sale price, selling online may be a better choice.

Q: Can I donate my wedding dress?

A: Yes, there are a variety of organizations that accept donated wedding dresses, such as Brides Across America and Wish Upon a Wedding.

Q: What should I do if I can't sell my wedding dress?

A: If you're unable to sell your wedding dress, you may want to consider donating it to a good cause or repurposing the fabric into something else.

Conclusion

Now that you know the options available to you, selling your wedding dress doesn't have to be a stressful experience. Whether you choose to sell it online, consign it at a local bridal shop, or sell it directly to a bridal shop, there are plenty of ways to recoup some of the cost and pass your dress on to a new owner.
