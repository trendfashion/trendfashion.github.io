What Not To Wear To An Indian Wedding

Written by Daniel Oct 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Indian weddings are a grand affair filled with colors, traditions, and celebrations. The attire worn by guests is an essential part of the wedding, and it is essential to dress appropriately for the occasion. However, it can be overwhelming to decide what to wear, and many guests make the mistake of wearing outfits that are inappropriate for the wedding. In this blog post, we will discuss what not to wear to an Indian wedding to help you avoid any fashion faux pas.

Attending an Indian wedding can be a daunting task, especially if you are not familiar with the culture and traditions. One of the most significant pain points for guests is deciding what to wear. Many guests opt for outfits that are either too revealing or not traditional enough, which can offend the hosts and other guests. It is crucial to keep in mind that Indian weddings are conservative affairs, and the attire should reflect this.

When attending an Indian wedding, it is best to avoid wearing black or white, as these colors are associated with mourning and funerals in Indian culture. It is also advisable to skip outfits that are too revealing, such as short dresses, low-cut blouses, or skirts with high slits. Avoid wearing jeans, t-shirts, or anything too casual, as Indian weddings are formal events.

In summary, what not to wear to an Indian wedding includes black or white outfits, revealing clothing, and casual attire. It is best to dress conservatively and choose outfits that are traditional and respectful of the culture and traditions.

Avoid Wearing Too Much Bling

When attending an Indian wedding, it is tempting to go all out with the bling. However, it is essential to remember that less is more when it comes to Indian weddings. Avoid wearing too much bling, as it can be overwhelming and take away from the beauty of the outfit. Instead, opt for subtle embellishments that complement the outfit.

Do Not Wear Shoes That are Uncomfortable

Indian weddings are long affairs, with ceremonies and rituals that can last for hours. It is essential to wear comfortable shoes that you can wear for an extended period. Avoid wearing heels that are too high or uncomfortable, as you will be on your feet for most of the time. Opt for flats or sandals that are comfortable and stylish.

Avoid Wearing Red

Red is a popular color for Indian weddings, especially for brides. It is best to avoid wearing red as it can be mistaken for the bride's outfit. Choose other colors that complement the theme of the wedding and are not too similar to the bride's outfit.

Wear Comfortable Fabric

Indian weddings often take place during the summer months, and the weather can be hot and humid. It is essential to wear comfortable fabric that is breathable and lightweight. Avoid wearing heavy fabrics or outfits that are too tight, as they can be uncomfortable and make you feel hot and stuffy.

Choose Appropriate Jewelry

Indian weddings are a great opportunity to wear traditional and beautiful jewelry. However, it is crucial to choose jewelry that complements the outfit and is not too overpowering. Avoid wearing too much jewelry or jewelry that clashes with the outfit. Opt for elegant and understated pieces that enhance the beauty of the outfit.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a saree or a salwar kameez to an Indian wedding?

A: Yes, you can wear a saree or a salwar kameez to an Indian wedding. These outfits are traditional and appropriate for the occasion.

Q: Can I wear black or white to an Indian wedding?

A: No, it is best to avoid wearing black or white to an Indian wedding, as these colors are associated with mourning and funerals in Indian culture.

Q: Can I wear jeans to an Indian wedding?

A: No, it is best to avoid wearing jeans to an Indian wedding, as this is too casual for the occasion. Indian weddings are formal events, and guests are expected to dress accordingly.

Q: Can I wear a low-cut blouse or a short dress to an Indian wedding?

A: No, it is best to avoid wearing revealing clothing to an Indian wedding. Indian weddings are conservative affairs, and it is essential to dress appropriately and respectfully.

Conclusion

In conclusion, attending an Indian wedding can be a fun and exciting experience, but it is essential to dress appropriately for the occasion. What not to wear to an Indian wedding includes black or white outfits, revealing clothing, and casual attire. It is best to dress conservatively and choose outfits that are traditional and respectful of the culture and traditions. Remember to keep it simple and elegant, and you will be sure to make a great impression on the hosts and other guests.
