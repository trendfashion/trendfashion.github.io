What Is Indian Headdress Called

Written by Frank Nov 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever come across an Indian with a fascinating headdress and wondered what it is called? Or have you been looking to buy one but don't know what to search for? The answer to your question is quite simple, and in this tutorial, we will explain what Indian headdress is called.

Indian headdresses are a significant part of Native American culture. They are worn for various reasons such as religious ceremonies, battles, and dances. However, cultural appropriation has led to the misuse of these headdresses by non-Natives, which has caused pain to the Native American community. It is essential to understand the significance of these headdresses and use them respectfully.

The Indian headdress is called a war bonnet or a feather headdress. War bonnets were worn by the Plains Indian tribes, while feather headdresses were worn by the other Native American tribes. The war bonnet is made of eagle feathers and animal fur, while the feather headdress is made of a range of feathers, including the eagle, hawk, and owl.

In summary, the Indian headdress is called a war bonnet or a feather headdress. It is an essential part of Native American culture and should be respected and used responsibly.

What is Indian Headdress Called and How to Choose the Right One?

Choosing the right Indian headdress depends on your needs and purpose. If you are looking for an authentic headdress, ensure that you buy from genuine Native American artisans. This will help support the Native American community and prevent cultural appropriation.

When buying a headdress, consider the type of feathers used, the length of the feathers, and the design. War bonnets are typically longer than feather headdresses and have eagle feathers. Feather headdresses can have a range of feathers and designs, depending on the tribe.

It is essential to note that Indian headdresses are not costumes and should not be used for Halloween or other events. They are a significant part of Native American culture and should be respected.

The Significance of Indian Headdress

The Indian headdress has a significant cultural and spiritual meaning in Native American tribes. It is a symbol of honor, bravery, and leadership. The headdress is worn by tribal leaders, warriors, and medicine men. It represents the connection between man and nature and the spiritual world.

However, the misuse of Indian headdresses has caused pain to the Native American community. Non-Natives wearing headdresses for fashion or costume purposes is disrespectful and considered cultural appropriation. It is important to understand and respect the significance of these headdresses to prevent further harm to the Native American community.

How to Wear an Indian Headdress?

If you are wearing an Indian headdress for cultural or spiritual purposes, it is essential to wear it correctly. The headdress should be worn with respect and honor. It should not be worn in a disrespectful or mocking manner.

When wearing the headdress, ensure that it is secured correctly and does not fall off. It should be worn with traditional Native American clothing and not with modern clothing or costumes.
