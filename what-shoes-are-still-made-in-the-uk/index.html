What Shoes Are Still Made In The Uk

Written by Petter Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you ever wonder if the shoes you're wearing were made in the UK? With the rise of fast fashion and global manufacturing, it can be difficult to know where your shoes come from. However, there are still shoes made in the UK, and this post will explore which ones.

It can be frustrating to want to support local industries and not know where to start. Additionally, many people have concerns about the ethics and environmental impact of the fashion industry. Buying shoes made in the UK can alleviate some of these concerns.

What Shoes are Still Made in the UK?

There are still several brands producing shoes in the UK. Some of these include Grenson, Trickers, and Church's. These brands are known for their high-quality craftsmanship and attention to detail. They often use traditional methods and materials, which can result in a higher price point, but also a longer-lasting shoe.

In addition to these high-end brands, there are also smaller companies producing shoes in the UK. These brands often focus on sustainability and ethical production, using materials like vegetable-tanned leather and recycled rubber.

My Experience with UK-Made Shoes

As someone who values sustainability and supporting local industries, I have sought out shoes made in the UK. I've purchased shoes from Grenson and a smaller brand called Po-Zu, which focuses on using sustainable materials. Both pairs of shoes have held up well and are comfortable to wear. The Grenson shoes, in particular, are a timeless style that I know will last for many years.

Why Buy Shoes Made in the UK?

There are several reasons to consider buying shoes made in the UK. First, supporting local industries can help to keep traditional skills and methods alive. It also reduces the environmental impact of shipping shoes around the world. Additionally, shoes made in the UK are often of higher quality and can last longer than cheaply-made alternatives.

The Future of UK-Made Shoes

While it's difficult to predict the future of any industry, there is hope that more companies will start producing shoes in the UK. The pandemic has led to a renewed interest in local industries, and consumers are becoming more aware of the environmental impact of fast fashion. Additionally, Brexit may lead to more companies choosing to manufacture in the UK to avoid trade barriers.

How to Find UK-Made Shoes

If you're interested in buying shoes made in the UK, there are a few ways to find them. First, research brands that produce shoes in the UK and seek out their products. You can also look for stores that specialize in sustainable or ethically-produced fashion. Finally, consider purchasing second-hand shoes made in the UK to reduce waste and support a circular economy.

Q&A

Q: Are UK-made shoes more expensive?

A: Generally, yes. Shoes made in the UK often use higher-quality materials and more traditional methods, which can result in a higher price point. However, many people find that the investment is worth it for a longer-lasting shoe.

Q: Are UK-made shoes more sustainable?

A: It depends on the brand and their production methods. Some UK-made shoes are produced with sustainability in mind, using eco-friendly materials and reducing waste. However, not all UK-made shoes are created equal, so it's important to do your research.

Q: Can I find UK-made shoes in chain stores?

A: It's unlikely that you will find UK-made shoes in chain stores, as they often prioritize lower-cost manufacturing options. However, some higher-end department stores may carry UK-made shoes from luxury brands.

Q: Are UK-made shoes comfortable?

A: Like any shoes, comfort depends on the brand and style. However, many people find that UK-made shoes are comfortable due to their attention to detail and use of high-quality materials.

Conclusion of What Shoes are Still Made in the UK

While the fashion industry can be overwhelming and difficult to navigate, buying shoes made in the UK can be a small step towards supporting local industries and reducing your environmental impact. Whether you're looking for a timeless luxury shoe or a sustainably-produced option, there are UK-made shoes out there for you.
