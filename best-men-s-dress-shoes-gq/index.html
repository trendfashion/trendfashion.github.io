The Classic Oxford

When it comes to dress shoes, the classic leather Oxford is a staple in any man's wardrobe. This timeless style is versatile and can be worn with a variety of formal looks, from suits to tuxedos. Brands like Allen Edmonds, Cole Haan, and Johnston & Murphy offer high-quality leather Oxfords that are both stylish and comfortable.

Personally, I have a pair of Cole Haan Oxfords that are my go-to for any formal event. They have a sleek design with a cap toe and are made with high-quality leather that molds to my feet over time. They're also surprisingly comfortable, even after hours of standing and dancing.

The Modern Loafer

If you're looking for a more modern take on dress shoes, consider a loafer. Brands like Gucci and Salvatore Ferragamo offer stylish loafers that can add a pop of personality to your formal look. Loafers are also a great option for those who prioritize comfort, as they often have more cushioning and support than traditional dress shoes.

Personally, I have a pair of Gucci loafers that I love to wear with a navy suit. They have a bold design with a gold horsebit detail and are made with soft leather that feels like butter on my feet. They're also surprisingly versatile and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion.

The Stylish Brogue

Brogues are another stylish option for dress shoes, particularly if you're looking for something with a bit more flair. These shoes feature decorative perforations and can come in a variety of colors and materials. Brands like Church's and Magnanni offer high-quality brogues that are both stylish and comfortable.

Personally, I have a pair of Magnanni brogues that I love to wear with a grey suit. They have a unique design with perforations in a wingtip pattern and are made with high-quality leather that feels great on my feet. They're also surprisingly lightweight, making them a great option for warmer weather.

How to Find the Right Fit

When it comes to finding the right fit for dress shoes, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, consider the shape of your foot. Those with wider feet may want to opt for a shoe with a wider toe box, while those with narrower feet may want to look for shoes with a tighter fit. It's also important to consider the length and width of the shoe, as well as the arch support and cushioning.

One tip for finding the right fit is to try on shoes in the afternoon or evening, as your feet tend to swell throughout the day. It's also a good idea to try on shoes with the socks you plan to wear with them, as this can affect the fit.
