How Much Do Interior Designers Make An Hour

Written by Christine Jun 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you interested in becoming an interior designer but concerned about the salary? Do you want to know how much interior designers make per hour? If so, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll explore the average hourly rate for interior designers and discuss factors that affect their pay.

Interior designers often face a variety of challenges that can impact their earnings. These include competition from other designers, fluctuating demand for their services, and the amount of experience they have in the field. Additionally, the location of the designer and the type of clients they work with can play a role in determining their hourly rate.

The average hourly wage for an interior designer in the United States is $25 per hour. However, this can vary widely depending on a number of factors. Experienced designers with a strong reputation and impressive portfolio can earn upwards of $100 per hour, while entry-level designers may earn closer to $15 per hour.

Personal Experience

As an interior designer with over 10 years of experience, I've seen firsthand how important it is to establish a strong reputation for quality work and excellent customer service. When I first started out, I charged around $20 per hour for my services. However, as I gained more experience and built up my portfolio, I was able to increase my hourly rate to $75 per hour.

Factors that Affect Hourly Rate

There are several factors that can impact how much an interior designer makes per hour. Some of the most important factors include:

Experience

The more experience an interior designer has, the higher their hourly rate is likely to be. Experienced designers have a track record of successful projects and satisfied clients, which can help them command higher fees.

Location

The location of the designer can also play a role in determining their hourly rate. Designers in major metropolitan areas, where the cost of living is higher, typically charge more for their services.

Education and Certification

Most interior designers have a degree in interior design or a related field. Those who have completed a formal education program and obtained certification from a professional organization like the National Council for Interior Design Qualification (NCIDQ) may be able to charge higher hourly rates than those without formal education or certification.

Portfolio and Reputation

A strong portfolio of successful projects and positive reviews from previous clients can help an interior designer command higher hourly rates. A designer with a proven track record of delivering high-quality work is likely to be in high demand and can set their hourly rate accordingly.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level interior designers make per hour?

A: Entry-level interior designers can expect to make between $15 and $25 per hour, depending on their location and level of experience.

Q: What is the average hourly rate for an experienced interior designer?

A: Experienced interior designers with a strong reputation and impressive portfolio can earn upwards of $100 per hour.

Q: Is certification necessary to become a successful interior designer?

A: While certification is not necessary to become an interior designer, it can help designers command higher hourly rates and establish credibility with clients.

Q: How can interior designers increase their hourly rate?

A: Interior designers can increase their hourly rate by gaining experience, building a strong portfolio, obtaining certification, and focusing on delivering high-quality work and excellent customer service.

Conclusion of How Much Do Interior Designers Make an Hour

Interior designers can make anywhere from $15 to $100 per hour, depending on a variety of factors such as experience, location, education, and reputation. By focusing on building their skills, establishing a strong reputation, and delivering excellent service to clients, interior designers can increase their hourly rate and achieve greater success in their field.
